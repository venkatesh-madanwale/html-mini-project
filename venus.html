Venus

About Venus

Size and structure: Venus is the second planet from the sun and the sixth largest planet in our solar system. It's similar in size and structure to Earth, with a radius of 6,052 km (3,760.5 miles) compared to Earth's 6,378 km (3,963 miles).
Temperature: Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system, with surface temperatures reaching 475° Celsius (900° Fahrenheit).
Atmosphere: Venus has a thick atmosphere made of carbon dioxide that traps heat in a runaway greenhouse effect. The surface pressure is more than 90 times that of Earth's.
Rotation: Venus spins slowly in the opposite direction from most planets, taking 243 Earth days to complete one rotation.
Orbit: Venus orbits the sun at an average distance of about 108 million km (67 million mi), and completes an orbit every 224.7 days.
Clouds: Venus's clouds are sulfuric acid.
Surface: Venus's surface is an inhospitable wasteland.
Name: Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty.
Brightness: Venus is the second brightest natural object in the night sky after the Moon.
History: The Babylonians recorded Venus's appearances around 3000 BCE, and it's also mentioned in the astronomical records of other ancient civilizations.
