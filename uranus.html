About Uranus

Uranus is the third largest planet in our solar system, with an equatorial diameter of 31,763 miles (51,118 kilometers).
Composition: Uranus is an ice giant made up of water, ammonia, and methane in a supercritical phase of matter.
Color: Uranus is blue-green in color due to the large amounts of methane that absorb red light and reflect blue back into space.
Rotation: Uranus rotates on its side, at a nearly 90-degree angle to the plane of its orbit.
Rings: Uranus has 13 faint rings.
Moons: Uranus has at least 27 moons, including Titania, Umbriel, Setebos, Miranda, and Puck.
Distance from the Sun: Uranus is about 19 astronomical units away from the Sun, which is about 1.8 billion miles (2.9 billion kilometers).
Temperature: Uranus is very cold, with an average temperature of -357° F (-216° C).
Wind: Uranus is very windy, with wind speeds reaching up to 560 miles per hour.
Discovery: Uranus was discovered by William Herschel on March 13, 1781.
