Saturn

About Saturn

Saturn is a gas giant planet and the second largest planet in our solar system:
Size: Saturn is about 10 times the diameter of Earth, with a radius about nine times that of Earth.
Composition: Saturn is made up mostly of hydrogen and helium.
Rings: Saturn is known for its complex and spectacular ring system.
Moons: Saturn has many moons, including Enceladus and Titan.
Density: Saturn is the least dense planet in our solar system, with a mean density of 0.69 g per cubic centimeter, making it less dense than water.
Distance from the Sun: Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and orbits it at an average distance of 1.4 billion kilometers.
Rotation: A day on Saturn lasts about 10.7 hours.
Orbit: A year on Saturn lasts about 29.5 Earth years.
