Mars

About Mars

Mars is known as the Red Planet because of the rusty dust that makes up its surface and atmosphere. However, close-up views show that Mars has many different colors, including butterscotch, golden, brown, tan, and even greenish.
Gravity Mars has about one-third of Earth's gravity, so a person who weighs 100 pounds on Earth would weigh about 38 pounds on Mars.
Atmosphere Mars' atmosphere is much thinner than Earth's and is made up of more than 95% carbon dioxide and less than 1% oxygen.
Dust storms Mars is prone to dust storms, which can sometimes cover the entire planet. These storms are often generated in the southern spring and summer.
Features Mars has polar ice caps, extinct volcanoes, canyons, and Valles Marineris, the largest valley in our solar system.
Time A day on Mars lasts 24.6 hours, and a year on Mars lasts 687 Earth days.
Location Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and one of Earth's closest planetary neighbors.
