About Earth

NASA has a lot of information about Earth, including its size, shape, atmosphere, and more: Size
Earth is the largest terrestrial planet and the fifth largest planet in our solar system. It has an equatorial diameter of 7,926 miles (12,760 kilometers). Shape
Earth is not a perfect sphere, but bulges at the equator due to its rotation. The diameter from the North to South Pole is 12,714 kilometers (7,900 miles),
while through the equator it is 12,756 kilometers (7,926 miles). Atmosphere
Earth's atmosphere is made up of mostly nitrogen and oxygen, and protects us from meteoroids.
Distance from the Sun Earth is one astronomical unit (AU) from the Sun, which is an average distance of 93 million miles (150 million kilometers).
Earth's rotation Earth's day is getting longer, increasing by about 1.7 milliseconds every century.
Earth's tilt Earth's axis is tilted at 23 degrees and 27 minutes relative to its orbital plane, which causes seasons.
Earth's mass Earth has a mass of approximately 6.5 x 10 tons (5.98 x 10 kg).
